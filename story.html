Our Story

Timeline

September 2023: Ellen Wang develops a vision of the Sunrise Venture Pitch Competition.

September 2023: Ellen Wang contacts the Irvine mayor Farrah Khan for support of the Sunrise Venture Pitch Competition.

October 2023: Mayor Khan provides a 500$ grant for the Sunrise Venture Pitch Competition.

November 2023: Ellen Wang creates the free, virtual Entrepreneurship Curriculum and Mentorship Program to make education accessible to all Californian students. The zoom curriculum features 9 different speakers at 10 sessions throughout February, March and April. The mentorship program features 9 entrepreneurs who will give students individualized pitch feedback. These programs connect students with the entrepreneurial community and open their network to meaningful relationships.

November 2023: The Sunrise team is recruited. Amador Valley junior Sunny Moon joins as the Chief Technology Officer and Publicist of NorCal, Northwood High junior Maya Iyengar joins as the Director of Outreach, Northwood High freshman Amber Chih joins as the Publicist of SoCal.

December 2024: University High junior Shuba Senthilkumar joins the Sunrise team as the Curriculum Coordinator.

December 2024: Sunrise Venture obtains various in-kind sponsorships from entrepreneurs, and sponsors include IP Lawyer Justin Sanders and entrepreneur Rudy Chavarria.

January 2024: Mayor Khan releases an official letter of support for SVPC*hyperlink.

February 2024: 120 students from 19 different high schools across California register for the Sunrise Venture Pitch Competition. Schools include Santa Monica High, Valencia High, Chaparral High, and OSCA.

February 2024: The Entrepreneurship Curriculum kicks off with 55 students in the first zoom session, “How to Craft a 90 Second Pitch”. The honored first speaker is David Ochi, the founding Executive Director of UCI ANTrepreneur Center and Executive Director of the Innovation Center at Cal State Dominguez Hills.

March 2024: The weekly curriculum + bi-weekly mentorship sessions take place. A total of 20 entrepreneurs, investors, and lawyers act as mentors, speakers, and judges, demonstrating the incredible supportiveness and unity of the OC entrepreneurial community.

April 2024: 90-second pitches are submitted and judges score teams’ pitches virtually, selecting top 10 finalists to compete in the second round. The final competition location and date are TBD in May or June.
