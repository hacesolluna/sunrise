John Russo

John Russo is a full-time faculty at Irvine Valley College in the School of Business Sciences and is the faculty lead for the Entrepreneurship Program where he has hosted and judged multiple pitch competitions. He has more than 20 years of sales, management, and marketing experience in a variety of industries such as wireless technology, advertising, mortgage lending, and consumer and packaged goods.

As an entrepreneur and business owner in the food industry, he successfully ran his own restaurant franchise and has an understanding of business ownership and entrepreneurial opportunities. He is currently a board member of Tech Coast Venture Network (TCVN), an entrepreneurship organization in Orange County.

Justin Sanders

Justin Sanders is an intellectual property attorney and managing partner at Master Key IP. For the past 17+ years, Justin has assisted clients (primarily startups and small businesses) with all aspects of identifying, protecting and monetizing their intellectual property in both the U.S. and abroad. Justin graduated from California State University, Long Beach, earning a B.S. in Computer Science with a minor in Human Resources Management.

He went on to attend Whittier Law School as a Fellow in the Center for Intellectual Property Law, where he received his J.D. with magna cum laude honors along with a Certificate in Intellectual Property Law. In addition to servicing clients through Master Key IP, Justin is actively involved with a number of local universities and startup organizations, where he serves as a resource and mentor to resident entrepreneurs, providing guidance on various intellectual property issues.

Shaun Sanders

Shaun is the Managing Partner of VentureCraft and leverages his 20+ years of experience in the startup world to help early-stage companies commercialize their technology and raise capital. His clients have raised more than $400 million to date.

Shaun also serves as the Senior Associate Director of one of the top SBDC-funded accelerators in the country, Octane LaunchPad. Additionally, Shaun is an Adjunct Professor of Law at USC Gould teaching business principles of law to attorneys and Masters' level students.

Vincent Moiso

Vincent Moiso is a principal and President of Oceanside Glass & Tile. He has been in the tile and flooring industry for 25 years with an international background in distribution and manufacturing. Vincent is also the founder and president of his own consulting and coaching practice focused primarily on small businesses. He has consulted for dozens of businesses advising on everything from organizational structure, strategic planning, to scaling and exiting a business.

Vincent published a new on-line course in 2019, the Complete Guide on Developing Tile & Stone Specifications with Architects in partnership with Ceramic Tile & Stone Consultants. He also recently published his book, How to Survive the Wilderness: The Playbook for the Entrepreneur and High Performer, which has been a top seller in several categories on Amazon.

Vincent is a certified DiSC and Lead With Purpose trainer, both tools that are emphasized in all of his coaching engagements. With an MBA from Cal State Fullerton and a BA in international relations from the University of San Diego, Vincent has also taught a business course for a decade at his alma mater where he serves as President of the Alumni Board of Directors for the University.

Chris Wagner

Dr. Chris Wagner has 25 years of experience developing technology for medical robotics and surgical visualization, both in academic and industry settings. His technology interests include novel sense-of-touch interfaces, MRI compatible actuators, small diameter robotics, and image guided robotics. He has worked for and co-founded startups in orthopedic planning and visualization, robotic-assisted joint replacement, and SPECT-CT image analysis.

Dr. Wagner has authored over 20 publications and holds multiple patents, and received his MS and PhD in Engineering Sciences from Harvard University. He is currently the co-founder and CTO of Current Surgical, developing technology for the minimally invasive treatment of unresectable liver tumors.

Dr. Cynthia West

Cynthia West, Ph.D. is the Director of the Leatherby Center for Entrepreneurship and Business Ethics and Associate Professor at Chapman University. She is also CEO of Rezultant Inc., a sales strategy and leadership consultancy. Cynthia was a key executive in 4 start-up technology companies, two of which had successful exits.

After 30 years in the technology industry as a sales leader, Cynthia returns to her roots in academia, to educate and collaborate with the next generation of entrepreneurs. Cynthia is a published author, public speaker, triathlete, foodie, spouse and mother of two.

Chris Daden

Chris Daden is a dynamic serial entrepreneur from Greater Los Angeles and has achieved remarkable success in Enterprise Technology and other industries, with multiple exits. With over $100M in software brought to market, Chris' innovative solutions have made a significant impact in global enterprises, including Fortune 500 companies.

Blending groundbreaking technological solutions with a deep commitment to business growth and mentorship, Chris Daden is reshaping industries alongside the next generation of tech leaders. Chris has extensive experience as both a founder and operator holding leadership roles as CEO and CTO, while serving on advisory and fiduciary boards.

Cynthia Kirkeby

Cynthia Kirkeby started her first company in college. She currently has 2 companies: IMPRINT Adtech, focusing on advertising technology, and Wiz-a-Witz, a S.T.E.A.M. educational game company. She was an Innovation Advisor and Advisory Board Member at UCI for the Masters of Innovation Entrepreneurship program. She has degrees in Graphic Design & Illustration and English (Writing). Her special topics include AI tools.

Jan Biermeyer

Jan went from coding at age 4 on the German country side to co-founding a startup in a Palo Alto garage, a robotics lab in South Korea and an autonomous car lab at UC Berkeley. He ran the Yahoo! foosball league, fixed espresso machines in Cal labs and was known throughout Germany for motivating vocational IT students with German chocolate during their exams. Jan also built a 350+ strong grass-roots data science community and first AI prototypes at a multi-trillion dollar fund.

In between, he managed to get two Masters, most of a Phd, and cofounded a health-tech startup acquired by Apple. More recently, Jan was moonlighting as fractional CTO for up to 8 concurrent tech startups, coaching a dozen founders, and mentoring dozens of high potential data science/AI/startup individuals.

Jan happily lives in Newport Beach with his son and wife, multiple surfboards and dozens of coffee gadgets. When he is not exploring the world with his toddler, Jan spends most of his free time being active along/on the ocean or tinkering in his evil lair garage.
