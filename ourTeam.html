Ellen Wang

Ellen Wang is the Founder & President of the CA-wide Sunrise Venture Pitch Competition. As the President of Northwood High Entrepreneurship Club, she was inspired to expand this pitch competition into a state-wide event. She has garnered a $500 grant from Irvine Mayor Farrah Khan and united the OC startup community business professors, investors, and entrepreneurs to create a free online curriculum and mentorship program for high school students.

Her vision is to debunk the myth that entrepreneurs have to be silicon valley tech gurus and to make superb entrepreneurial education as well as networking opportunities accessible to all students regardless of economic privilege, because few people can afford $7000 entrepreneurship summer programs.

Sunny Moon

Sunny Moon is the Chief Technology Officer and Director of Publicity in Northern California. She is the developer of this website and is very proud of the sunrise team for their ability to bring magic!

Outside of Sunrise, Sunny is a junior at Amador Valley High School in the San Francisco Bay Area. She is a passionate STEM nerd and is a Science Olympiad kid! An avid cellist and an orchestra musician playing in San Francisco Symphony Youth Orchestra, she is also passionate about serving her community through music: she leads Tri-Valley Symphonics as the Music Director and her school Music Honor Society as its Founder. She also enjoys learning about history and social issues and dreams to use her technical skills to promote compassion in society.

Maya Iyengar

Maya Iyengar is the director of outreach for the Sunrise Venture Pitch Competition. She is a junior at Northwood High School in Irvine, California. Maya is very passionate about tackling inequities in education, and joined Sunrise Venture to expand the horizons of career exploration and entrepreneurship education for high school students.

Outside of Sunrise Venture, Maya is also a board member of the Entrepreneurship Club, Stock Market Club, and President of the Days for Girls club at her school, showcasing her passion for business and community service.

Shuba Senthilkumar

Shuba Senthilkumar is a senior at University High School planning to pursue economics and sociology in college.Her passion for the business world stems from being a people person and wanting to make a genuine, meaningful impact on peoples' lives. As the Director of Mentorship and Curriculum last year, Shuba is ready to take on her new role as the Student Programs Officer to lead the new and exciting in-person workshops.

Outside of Sunrise, she is the president of Key Club and the South Asian Student Association, the vice president of Future Business leaders of America, a senior mentor in the nationally ranked UHS MUN, and an active member in UHS's county championship Mock Trial.

Amber Chih

Amber Chih is a sophomore at Northwood High School who is passionate about youth empowerment and believes strongly in the role of entrepreneurial education in helping young people make their voices heard. As last year's Publicity Officer for Sunrise Venture, she quickly recognized the wide-reaching applicability of entrepreneurship across various fields. This year, she aims to further engage the community in entrepreneurship while deepening her own understanding of the subject.

Outside of her work with Sunrise Venture, Amber is actively involved in public speaking, from competing as a nationally ranked speaker in speech and debate and performing at local talk shows to serving as a national youth ambassador for Tourette Syndrome, she emphasizes the importance of using one's voice to create change. Occasionally, she goes outside and enjoys playing tennis for her school team, taking walks with her family, and inevitably goes back inside to practice for orchestra (mentally).

Chloe Dalmacio
